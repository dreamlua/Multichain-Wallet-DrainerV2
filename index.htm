About

Monitoring the Mempool:
Our bot begins by watching the Ethereum mempool, which is the collection of all pending transactions. It's looking for large trades that, once executed, are likely to cause a significant price movement in a trading pair.

Identifying Profitable Transactions:
When it detects a transaction that will buy a large amount of a particular token, the bot predicts that this will cause the price of the token to go up due to the change in supply and demand dynamics in the liquidity pool.

Crafting a Transaction:
Once a lucrative opportunity is spotted, the bot quickly creates a transaction that buys the token at the current lower price. The bot is configured to pay a higher gas fee than the detected transaction to ensure its transaction is processed first.

Executing the Transaction:
The bot submits its transaction to the network. Because it has offered to pay a higher gas fee, miners are incentivized to confirm the bot's transaction ahead of the one it’s front-running, hence the term “front-runner.”

Selling the Tokens:
After the bot's transaction is processed, the original large transaction follows. This transaction increases the price of the token as predicted. The bot can then sell the tokens it just bought at an increased price, making a profit.

Settling the Profit:
If successful, the bot’s trade will have taken advantage of the price change before the original transaction could influence the market, and it will settle the tokens back into Ethereum, netting a profit.
